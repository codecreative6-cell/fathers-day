Page Eight · Off The Record

For the man
who made us

You were the first voice that told me the world was worth meeting. The first hands that held me like I mattered — before I’d done a single thing to deserve it.

You taught me to shake a hand and mean it. To lose without falling apart. To show up — not for applause, not for credit, just because that’s what love does when no one’s watching.

I see it now, in the silences you kept so I could speak. In the worries you carried so mine could feel small. In the way you made ordinary mornings feel like something worth waking up for.

I carry you in the way I face the world.
And I am grateful — more than I’ll ever say out loud.

Thank you, Dad.

With love
